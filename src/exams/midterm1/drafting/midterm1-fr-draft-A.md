## FRQ1

Consider the market for widgets, with demand and supply given by:

Demand: \( Q_D = 24 - 2P \) 
Supply: \( Q_S = 12 + 2P \)

Answer the following:

a) Sketch the supply and demand diagram. Be sure to label the *price intercepts* (i.e., the intercepts on the vertical axis, where \(Q=0\)) of both curves.

b) Solve for the equilibrium price, \( P^* \). Show your work.

c) Suppose the government imposes a per-unit tax of \( 2 \) on sellers. What is the new equilibrium quantity, \( Q_t \)?

d) With the tax in place, what price do buyers pay, \( P_b \)?

e) Does the tax burden fall more on buyers or sellers, or is it split evenly? (That is, compare the change in \(P_b\) to the change in \(P_s\).) Briefly explain.

## FRQ2

Two firms, A and B, produce the same good. Their marginal cost tables are:

| Q | MC_A | MC_B |
|---|------|------|
| 0 | 0    | 0    |
| 1 | 1    | 2    |
| 2 | 2    | 4    |
| 3 | 3    | 6    |
| 4 | 4    | 8    |
| 5 | 5    | 10   |

a) First suppose the market price is \( P=4 \). How many units does each firm supply? (According to our class convention, assume each firm produces the quantity that maximizes profit, producing each unit for which \(MC \le P\) and stopping when the next unit would have \(MC>P\)).

b) Instead suppose the total quantity to be produced is fixed at \( Q_A + Q_B = 6 \).  
If production is efficient (total cost minimized), what are \( Q_A \) and \( Q_B \)? Explain briefly.
