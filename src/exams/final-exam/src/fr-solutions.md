# ECON 1001 Final Exam: Free Response Solutions

## Problem 28: Market Power & Price Discrimination

### **Version A**

**Parameters:** Aggregate Demand: $P = 10 - 0.5Q$ | $MC = 4$

**(a) Single Price & Quantity**

- Set $MR = MC$ to find $Q^*$.
- **$Q^* = 6$**
- Plug $Q^*$ into Aggregate Demand to find $P^*$.
- **$P^* = \$7$**

**(b) Deadweight Loss (DWL)**

- Efficient Quantity ($Q_{eff}$) is where $P = MC \rightarrow Q = 12$.
- $DWL = \frac{1}{2} \times (P^* - MC) \times (Q_{eff} - Q^*)$
- $DWL = 0.5 \times (7 - 4) \times (12 - 6)$
- **$DWL = 9$**

**(c) Total Profit at Single Price**

- Profit $= (P^* - MC) \times Q^*$
- Profit $= (7 - 4) \times 6$
- **Profit $= \$18$**

**(d) Price if firm CANNOT distinguish groups**

- If the firm cannot distinguish, they face the aggregate demand curve.
- They will charge the same single price found in part (a).
- **Price $= \$7$**

**(e) Price Discrimination (Distinguishing Groups)**

- Act as a monopolist in each market: Set $MR = MC$ for Locals and Visitors.
- **Locals:** $P_L = 12 - Q_L \rightarrow$ Set $MR_L = MC \rightarrow Q_L = 4 \rightarrow$ **$P_L = \$8$**
- **Visitors:** $P_V = 8 - Q_V \rightarrow$ Set $MR_V = MC \rightarrow Q_V = 2 \rightarrow$ **$P_V = \$6$**
- **New Total Profit:** $(P_L - MC) \times Q_L + (P_V - MC) \times Q_V = (8 - 4) \times 4 + (6 - 4) \times 2$
- **Profit $= \$20$**

**(f) Does the socially efficient price change?**

- **No.** The socially efficient price is always where Marginal Benefit equals Marginal Cost ($P = MC$).
- Because the marginal cost remains $\$4$ for both groups, the socially efficient price remains $\$4$. Price discrimination changes the *profit-maximizing* prices, but not the *socially efficient* outcome.
